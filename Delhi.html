📋 Executive Summary

This document provides a comprehensive mapping of the medical imaging landscape in North India, including major hospital groups, diagnostic chains, standalone imaging centers, government hospitals, Public-Private Partnership (PPP) projects, open tenders, and government schemes and policies.

For potential bidders, the key takeaways are:

  1. Focus on PPP Models – States like Delhi, Haryana, Punjab, and Maharashtra are actively using PPPs to scale diagnostic services, offering significant opportunities for service providers and equipment suppliers.
  2. Leverage Government Schemes – PLI subsidies, capital subsidies for components, and missions like the Maha MedTech Mission offer direct financial support to manufacturers.
  3. Be Prepared for Evolving Regulations – GTE rules are dynamic, and eligibility may vary by tender. Always verify the latest position.

💰 Part IX: Government Schemes & Policies

Production Linked Incentive (PLI) Scheme for Medical Devices
Outlay ₹3,420 Cr; period FY2022‑23 to FY2026‑27; 5% incentive on incremental sales. Performance (Sep 2025): cumulative eligible sales ₹12,344.37 Cr (exports ₹5,869.36 Cr). 21 projects manufacturing 54 unique devices including MRI, CT, Cath Lab. Impact: domestic devices 10‑30% cheaper.
Reference: PIB Release Dec 2025; Medical Buyer Feb 2026
Maha MedTech Mission
Launched by ANRF, in collaboration with ICMR and Gates Foundation, Government of Maharashtra. Funding ₹5‑25 Cr per project (up to ₹50 Cr exceptional). Eligible: academic & R&D institutions, hospitals, startups, MSMEs, MedTech industry. Focus: advanced diagnostic imaging, minimally invasive, point‑of‑care, AI/ML.
Reference: PIB Release Nov 2025
Capital Subsidy for Component Manufacturing
20% capital subsidy for manufacturing components for digital X‑ray, CT, and MRI. Target: MSMEs producing detectors, coils, power supplies, etc. Lowers entry barrier for domestic component manufacturing.
Reference: ET HealthWorld Aug 2024
Global Tender Enquiry (GTE) Exemption – Evolving Landscape
354 medical devices exempt from local procurement clause under Rule 161(iv) of GFRs 2017, valid until March 31, 2027 unless otherwise ordered. DoP sought stakeholder comments on amended list until Feb 28, 2026. Import of refurbished medical devices banned (Economic Times Jan 2025). Strategic implication: Check each tender for GTE vs Limited.
References: Medical Buyer (Feb 2026); Pharmabiz (Feb 2026)

🎯 Part XII: Strategic Takeaways for Potential Bidders

#TakeawayActionable Insight
1Focus on PPP ModelsStates like Delhi, Haryana, Punjab, and Maharashtra actively using PPPs to scale diagnostic services – significant opportunity for private service providers and equipment suppliers.
2Leverage Government SchemesPLI subsidies (5% on incremental sales), 20% capital subsidy for components, Maha MedTech Mission (₹5‑25 Cr funding) offer direct financial support to manufacturers.
3Be Prepared for Evolving GTE RegulationsGTE rules dynamic; some categories may favour domestic suppliers, others open to global competition. Stay updated on DoE exemption list.
4Understand the Comprehensive Service ModelMany state PPP projects are comprehensive service agreements covering financing, infrastructure, equipment, long‑term maintenance (10+ years), staffing, and SLAs with KPIs.
5Leverage "Make in India" for Competitive AdvantageDomestic medical devices 10‑30% cheaper due to PLI. Partner with or prioritize local manufacturers to become cost competitive.
6Target Specific High‑Value OpportunitiesMonitor e‑procurement portals of Delhi, Punjab, Maharashtra, Haryana for large‑scale PPP tenders. Success in Pune likely to replicate.
7Monitor GTE Rules CloselyRegularly check updates from Department of Expenditure (DoE) regarding GTE exemption list – determines if tender is open globally or reserved for local players.
