Medical Terminology for Technical Support Engineers

Essential terms for interacting with clinical customers and stakeholders

Positioning Terminology

Anterior

Of or involving the front of the main body.

Clicking on the "A" in the 3D viewport aligns the image in an anterior position.

Posterior

Of or involving the back of the main body.

Clicking on the "P" aligns the 3D image in a posterior position.

Inferior

Indicating lower than a given reference point.

Clicking "I" views the image from an inferior aspect of the patient.

Superior

Indicating higher than a given reference point.

Clicking "S" views the image from a superior aspect of the patient.

Right Lateral

Pertaining to the right side of the body.

Clicking "R" aligns the image in a right lateral position.

Left Lateral

Pertaining to the left side of the body.

Clicking "L" aligns the image in a left lateral position.

Supine

Posterior recumbent body position where the patient lies on their back.

In Vitrea, determine if a patient is supine by looking at the axial MPR.

Prone

Anterior recumbent body position where the patient lies on their stomach.

In Vitrea, determine if a patient is prone by looking at the axial MPR.

Body Structure Terminology

Carotid

Arteries that supply blood to the head and neck.

The common carotid artery divides into external and internal carotid arteries.

Cardiac

Having to do with the heart.

Vitrea AV includes Cardiac Arteries, Cardiac CA Scoring, and Cardiac Functional Analysis applications.

Coronary

Relating to arteries which surround and supply the heart.

The Cardiac Arteries Application looks for plaque in the coronary arteries.

Aorta

The major artery that carries oxygenated blood from the heart.

Vitrea AV applications like Vascular Aorta and Vascular TAVR assess the aorta.

Miscellaneous Terminology

Perfusion

The passage of blood through blood vessels in an organ.

Vitrea AV measures perfusion in Brain Perfusion CT or Body Perfusion CT applications.

Subtraction

Removal of specific anatomical structures to see vessels.

Vitrea AV has Head Subtraction and Coronary Subtraction applications.

ROI

Region of Interest: An area on a study that the user is interested in.

ROIs can be elliptical or freehand and drawn in any Vitrea AV application.

Segmentation

The removal of specific anatomical structures using software.

Users might segment the liver to get a volume, removing it from the rest of the study.

DICOM

Digital Imaging and Communication System standard.

DICOM is the standard for communication and management of medical imaging information.
