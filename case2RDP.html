📌 Executive Brief (One‑page summary)

▼

a. The Problem: A Legacy RDP Protocol is creating a compliance gap — Our RDP‑based delivery architecture has become the primary reason for tender disqualifications in Southeast Asia. RDP is inherently incompatible with mandatory "Zero Trust" standards, resulting in protocol‑level vulnerabilities.

b. Market/Financial Impact: $XM at risk (WIP). Failure to act will immediately lose all advanced visualization tenders in Singapore. SingHealth withholds milestone payments and threatens legal action for non‑compliance.

c. Options Considered:
• Improve Current Architecture: low impact, does not solve Zero Trust gap.
• ZTNA / VDI Architecture (Recommended): replaces RDP with managed zero‑trust platform, closes compliance gaps.

d. Risk Analysis:
• Status Quo (Do Nothing): High risk — continued disqualification, loss of regional share.
• Adopt New Architecture: medium investment, high return, aligns with market requirements.

e. Recommendation: Review and prioritize the development of a reference architecture based on current market requirements, with the goal of replacing RDP with a modern, vendor‑managed delivery platform.

📄 Detailed Executive Summary

▼

Canon is losing competitive ground in Southeast Asia, particularly in Singapore, because our advanced visualization solution continues to rely on RDP (port 3389) for thin‑client communication.

The current foundational technology we rely upon to deliver application access has been widely deprecated for healthcare use.

Healthcare security standards now mandate Zero Trust architecture – a model that RDP is fundamentally incompatible with. Competitors (Siemens, GE, Philips, AGFA) have moved to web‑native, cloud‑native, or zero‑trust architectures that do not use RDP. Canon remains one of the few vendors still exposing customers to the risks of a legacy protocol.

Competitors with modern, secure delivery models are exploiting this gap, resulting in disqualification from tenders (Parkway, NUH) and legal liability on the SingHealth accounts. The sole issue is that RDP is no longer an acceptable delivery protocol for healthcare due to protocol‑level insecurity and Zero Trust incompatibility.

This case study outlines RDP's technical vulnerabilities, compliance gap, regulatory non‑alignment, quantifies business impact, and requests Global PM to adopt an alternative delivery architecture.

In summary: Siemens, GE, Philips, AGFA moved away from RDP or abstracted it. Canon remains fully exposed to native RDP vulnerabilities – the exception in the market.

Canon's use of RDP has exposed it to CVEs that competitors avoided by migrating to modern architectures. These vulnerabilities relate directly to the underlying Microsoft RDP protocol. Competitors either moved away from RDP entirely or invested heavily in mitigation.

e. How a Vendor‑Owned architecture fixes this

Core RequirementBuilt‑In Capability of ZTNA / VDI PlatformResolves Compliance Gap
Network cloaking / no open inbound portsZero Trust platforms expose no inbound ports; outbound/encrypted trafficEliminates RDP‑on‑port‑3389 exposure – primary disqualification reason
Least‑privilege accessGranular policies restrict users to specific application, time‑bound sessionsRemoves lateral movement risk that RDP allows
DICOM / high‑performance graphicsCitrix HDX 3D lossless codec, AWS WorkSpaces for syngo.viaVendor‑supported reference architecture, no RDP required

The shift is fundamental: Instead of delivering RDP and hoping the hospital hardens it, Canon would deliver a full stack – managed ZTNA or VDI platform – with network cloaking, least‑privilege, and medical graphics without RDP.

f. Validation – This approach already exists

The current architecture does not include a zero‑trust or full VDI layer in the standard offering. Hospitals can deploy these layers themselves, but Singapore/SEA tenders increasingly require a vendor‑delivered turnkey architecture. Providing such architecture means Canon selects, licenses, deploys and supports the entire remote access stack.

🧭 The Path Forward: Two Strategic Choices for Global PM

▼
  1. Acknowledge that RDP is the fundamental bottleneck in SEA.
  2. Adopt at least one alternative delivery architecture for SEA markets where RDP is unacceptable:
    • Zero‑Trust Network Access (ZTNA) – like Siemens.
    • Web/Native client – like Philips.
    • Cloud‑native SaaS – like GE.
    RDP cannot provide any of these out of the box.
  3. Request formal competitive architecture analysis comparing RDP vs. web‑native/zero‑trust for customer discussions.
We need a clear commitment from Global PM to develop and validate an alternative delivery architecture within a specific timeline, or acknowledge that we will not be able to compete in SEA for advanced visualization tenders.

✅ Conclusion

▼

The RDP architecture is no longer sustainable for healthcare advanced visualization in Singapore and broader SEA. Without a modern compliant alternative, we will continue to be disqualified from tenders and lose existing accounts to competitors who have already transitioned. The risk is immediate and the market gap widening.

This case study establishes that the proposed path forward is not incremental but a competitive necessity. Global Product Management is requested to provide clarity, resources, and a timeline for transitioning to an architecture that can compete in today's compliance‑driven healthcare environment.
